Flu Kills

Each year, thousands of patients catch the flu from their doctors and nurses.



  It's Time to Vaccinate Doctors  

The Flu Kills Patients

Though you might only miss work, the flu is serious for vulnerable hospital patients



Newborns

Catching the flu in the hospital can kill newborns — That's one reason why Ob-Gyns take the flu very seriously.

Cancer Patients

Chemotherapy drugs weaken the immune system, meaning the flu can kill otherwise strong and young people.

The Elderly

Our grandparents are the most likely to die from the flu, which is the cause of 1 in 20 deaths in people over 65

Reduce Hospitalizations

The flu causes 200,000 hospitalizations each year. During peak flu times, emergency rooms are frequently overwhelmed.

Prevent Deaths

The flu causes 36,000 deaths each year, all of which are preventable. Influenza is the sixth leading cause of death among US adults.

Herd Immunity

Currently, less than 50% of doctors and nurses get the vaccine — well below the 80–90% required for "herd immunity" to protect others.



Saves Medical Costs

With rising healthcare costs, requiring flu vaccines for doctors & nurses is a great way to reduce expenses.

Supported by Key Groups

Requiring flu shots for doctors & nurses is supported by every major medical association and hundreds of hospitals.

Other Vaccines

We already require many other vaccines for doctors & nurses, including measles, mumps, rubella, and hepatits B. It's time to include the flu.

Ask Your Doctor or Nurse

Check that your doctor & nurse have gotten the flu vaccine before you make an appointment. Ask their secretaries too — anyone in the office could give you and loved ones the flu.

Tell Your Friends & Family

Know someone battling cancer? Make sure their oncologist is flu-free. Have a pregnant friend? Remind them to check that their Ob-Gyn is vaccinated.

Call Your Government

The flu shot isn't a political issue — it's a public health issue! Ask your local and state representatives about requiring health care professionals to get the flu shot.
